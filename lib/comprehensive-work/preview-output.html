Android–Gynoid Ratio

YOUR A/G RATIO
1.3
Also known as Waist-to-Hip ratio

What is A/G ratio?

The ratio of fat percentage in your android (abdominal) region to your gynoid (hip & thigh) region. We use fat % rather than inches because muscle can distort measurements.

Why it matters

A value below 1.0 is associated with desirable body shapes (pear, hourglass) and lower visceral fat. Above 1.0 correlates with apple / round shapes and higher visceral-fat risk.

Causes of imbalance

Movement and physical function require a balance between muscle tone, strength and length. Repeated movements or sustained postures can set up a muscle imbalance that may lead to joint dysfunction.

How does it affect you?

Many musculoskeletal conditions are caused by muscle imbalance between opposing regions. These imbalances could cause an injury, or contribute to back pain from overuse of some muscles.

How to improve?

  • Talk to a physiotherapist
  • Stretching exercises
  • Build muscle in weaker regions
  • Correct your posture
  • Reduce overall body fat

T-Score & Z-Score

T-Score compares your bone mass to a healthy young adult of your gender. Z-Score compares it to an average person in your age group.

When to be concerned

Osteoporosis: T-score < −2.5 — consult a doctor. Osteopenia: T-score between −1 and −2.5 indicates weaker-than-normal bone.

How to improve

• Sufficient calcium (1000–1200 mg/day)
• Vitamin D 600–800 IU/day
• Weight-bearing exercise
• Avoid smoking and excess alcohol
